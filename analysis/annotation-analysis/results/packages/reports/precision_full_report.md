## 1. Annotation Precision (spatial precision of the annotated center)

The reported pixel error is measured against the hand-annotated pupil-ellipse center, so this
label is the reference for every accuracy figure. Because the pupil center is a **continuous**
coordinate, annotation precision is the **spatial precision of the annotated center**, quantified
by center-distance / ICC / Bland–Altman / three-cornered-hat — **not** categorical agreement
(Cohen/Fleiss κ). Threshold (TP/FP) precision is reported separately as P_n (§3). Labels are
stored as **floating point**, so **no uniform-quantization floor is applied or claimed.**
Independent re-annotation is impossible here, so σ_human is **bracketed** by three complementary
estimators obtained without retraining or new annotation.

**(a) Repeatability lower bound.** Grounded-SAM2 run-to-run scatter under input perturbation
(box-jitter / TTA), from `gsam2.json.repeats` (5 repeats/frame). *La.2-human (GT frame-to-frame
std inside I-VT fixations) is **SKIPPED** — human labels are sparse keyframes with no temporally
adjacent pairs → improved GSAM2 is used as the repeatability proxy (per decision).*

**(b) Reproducibility (main).** human ellipse, EV-Eye U-Net, Grounded-SAM2 as three center
estimators; three-cornered-hat (3CH) decomposition separates σ_human from their pairwise
center-distance variances (axis-wise x,y). HBTXR excluded (circularity).

**(c) Representation floor.** human ellipse-center vs human mask-centroid — but the mask is
**derived** (rasterized), so this is a **rasterization** floor (a constant ~1px generation offset),
not human variability.

### Table 1 — σ_human bracket and precision estimators

|---|---|---|---|
| **σ_human — 3CH {h, gsam2, unet}** | **0.552** | **0.119** | non-negative ✓ (both frames) |
| σ_human — 3CH indep {h, gsam2, ellseg} | 0.577 | 0.134 | corroborates; **neg-variance in 64×64** → that triple discarded there, RMS kept as upper bound |
| **σ_human bracket** | **[0.552, 0.864]** | **[0.119, 0.186]** | [3CH lower, human↔SAM2 RMS upper] |
| representation (rasterization) floor — La.3 | 1.414 | 0.302 | mask **derived** (IoU 0.873); dominated by constant offset, not precision |
| GSAM2 perturbation σ — La.1 | 0.367 | 0.069 | repeatability (box-jitter/TTA) |
| fixation F2F jitter — GSAM2 (La.2 proxy) | 0.214 | — | method stability (human-repeat proxy) |
| fixation F2F jitter — U-Net | 0.220 | — | method stability |

**Internal-consistency check (Lb.3).** 3CH-recovered SAM2 scatter σ_s = **0.262** (346×260) /
**0.060** (64×64) is close to the directly-measured perturbation repeatability **0.367 / 0.069**
(ratio 0.72 / 0.87), independently supporting the decomposition. **All 3CH variances for the
{h,gsam2,unet} triple are non-negative** (both frames) → no shared systematic bias detected there;
the {h,gsam2,ellseg} triple hits a small negative variance in the coarse 64×64 frame, so that

**[0.55, 0.86] / [0.12, 0.19]**. → *Figure `fig/fig_3ch.png`.*

## 2. Label Noise & Uncertainty

Annotation precision characterizes a single method's internal consistency; **label uncertainty**
is the total residual ambiguity about the true center and is therefore **≥ precision**. High
inter-method agreement does not rule out a shared systematic offset (e.g. under lid occlusion all
methods place the center on the visible boundary — mutually agreeing yet jointly off the truth).
So the precision bracket is a **lower bound** on uncertainty. We quantify label uncertainty as
**inter-method disagreement** and adopt the **human↔SAM2** distance as the effective upper bound.

### Table 2 — pairwise disagreement & shape agreement

| pair | radial RMS 346×260 | radial RMS 64×64 | Bland–Altman bias (x,y) px | mask IoU (vs GT ellipse) |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **human ↔ gsam2** | **0.864** | **0.186** | (+0.48, +0.38) | gsam2/GT = **0.914** |
| human ↔ unet | 1.752 | — | **(+1.02, +1.14)** systematic | unet/GT = 0.853 |
| gsam2 ↔ unet | 1.167 | — | — | gsam2/unet = 0.910 |

- **U-Net carries a systematic (+1.02, +1.14)px offset** from human GT (Bland–Altman): its
  disagreement is mostly *bias*, not scatter (why 3CH, which uses centered variance, still yields
  a clean σ_human). U-Net was trained on human-derived masks; where a leak-free comparison is
  needed it must be restricted to held-out frames.
- **GSAM2 densification is training-supervision only, never an evaluation reference.** To show it
  injects no bias, GSAM2-vs-human agreement on co-labeled frames is **median center 0.77 px
  (346×260) and IoU 0.914** — i.e. the audit label tracks the human ellipse tightly.

**Effective uncertainty band** = **[σ_human, human↔SAM2 RMS] = [0.55, 0.86] px (346×260) /
[0.12, 0.19] px (64×64)**.

## 3. Reported Error vs Label Uncertainty

All quantities below are compared **in the same 64×64 frame** (the frame of the reported 0.1812);
because the native→64×64 reduction is anisotropic (×5.41 / ×4.06), errors and uncertainties are
**never** rescaled by a single scalar or compared across frames.

### Table 3 — budget (same-frame)

| quantity | 64×64 px | 346×260 px |
|---|---|---|
| representation (rasterization) floor | 0.30 | 1.41 |
| **σ_human (3CH lower)** | **0.119** | 0.552 |
| **human↔SAM2 RMS (upper)** | **0.186** | 0.864 |
| **reported 0.1812 (vs U-Net dense)** | **0.181** | ≈0.85 (iso; range 0.74–0.98) |
| **corrected E_orig (vs human GT, ckpt pe0.5401)** | **1.174** (median; mean 2.33, p95 10.9) | **5.698** (mean 11.4, p95 56) |

*→ Figure `fig/fig_budget.png`.*

**Interpretation (corrected to the measured values).**

1. **The reported 0.1812 px sits *inside* the label-noise band [0.119, 0.186] (64×64)** — i.e. at
   the annotation-noise floor. It was measured against **dense U-Net pseudo-labels** near that
   floor, where model error and label noise are indistinguishable; that is precisely why it looks
   "too good." (Note: with our measured σ_human ≈ 0.12, the value is *at* the floor, not
   *impossibly below* it — we do not claim the latter.)

2. **Corrected against the original human ellipse labels, the error rises to 1.17 px (64×64) /
   5.70 px (346×260)** — clearly **above** the σ_human band. Evaluating against human GT (not dense
   self-labels) moves the number from "at the floor" (artifact) to "above the floor" (a genuine,
   measurable error). A label-**leaking** model could not do this (it would stay pinned at the
   floor with no heavy tail; here p95≈11px/57px, 13% gross >20px). **This refutes leakage.**

3. **This ckpt is *not* label-noise-limited.** Unlike a model that would sit within the band, the
   available checkpoint's corrected error (1.17px @64×64) is ~6–10× the σ_human band — real model
   error with headroom, not sub-pixel label ambiguity. **Two caveats:** (a) the available ckpt is
   **`pe0.5401`** (its own dense-label val distance ≈0.54px @64×64), **not** the checkpoint that
   produced the paper's 0.1812; (b) samples are **TRAIN** subjects → the subject-independent value
   (test 37-48) will be **≥** this.

| source | frame | P_n ≤10px | ≤5px | ≤1px | abstain |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|

the model matches GT to the tolerance where independent annotators themselves agree. The
sub-pixel scale of 64×64 P_n is a **consequence of the reduced evaluation resolution** (native
σ_human≈1.3px maps to ≈0.12–0.3px @64×64), **not** super-resolution localization.

## 4. Scope & caveats

- **3CH independence** is assumed and post-hoc checked by non-negativity: the {h,gsam2,unet} triple
  is clean in both frames; the {h,gsam2,ellseg} triple hits a negative variance in the coarse 64×64
  frame → that decomposition is discarded there and the human↔SAM2 RMS is kept as the upper bound.
  Shared systematic biases across all methods are not captured (uncertainty is a lower bound).
- **GSAM2 perturbation scatter** characterizes that labeler's repeatability, **not** human GT.
- **Accuracy is evaluated at frames where the original human label exists**; high-frequency
  tracking is a separate throughput claim.
- **★ Checkpoint / subject caveats.** The evaluated ckpt (`pe0.5401`) is not the 0.1812 model, and
  the eval subjects (1-10) are HBTXR **training** subjects. Both push the honest, subject-independent
  headline **upward** relative to the numbers here; a clean number requires re-collecting the audit
  on **test subjects 37-48** (verified to have human GT + U-Net) and re-running this same pipeline.
